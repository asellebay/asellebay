Multi-Sport Pause & Resume UI

UX Design for Garmin Devices – by Aselle Bay

Overview

This UX project focuses on improving the flexibility of Garmin’s multi-sport activity tracking based on both personal experience and user research. While Garmin offers features like "Resume Later" or "Change Sport," they’re either hidden behind complex menus or limited to certain devices.

Problem

Switching between activities during workouts is often frustrating. Currently, the Garmin app requires ending one session before starting another, which breaks data continuity and disrupts the workout flow. Many users—including myself—experience confusion and data loss when trying to pause one activity (like biking), switch to a different one (like running), and then return to the first. Existing features like “Change Sport” or “Resume Later” are either hidden, inconsistent across devices, or don’t fully solve the problem.

Objectives

  • Enable seamless pausing and resuming of multiple activities without losing data
  • Simplify switching between sports during workouts with clear, easy-to-find controls
  • Ensure the interface is intuitive and efficient on small wearable screens
  • Maintain accurate and continuous tracking across activity transitions

Design Approach

  • Researched workflows of triathletes and multi-sport users
  • Mapped pain points in Garmin’s existing flow
  • Created UI mockups and interaction flows tailored for wearable devices

Key Features

  • “Pause & Switch” action: preserves session and context
  • Clear visual cues for current vs paused activity
  • Simple toggle to resume original activity seamlessly

Impact

The redesigned flow made it simple to switch activities, kept users feeling good about their workouts, and gave multi-sport athletes a smoother, more hassle-free Garmin experience.
