Starbucks Mobile App UX Redesign

Overview

In this project, I reimagined the Starbucks mobile app with a user-centered approach. The redesign focuses on simplifying navigation, reducing friction in the ordering process, and enhancing visual hierarchy — all while aligning closely with Starbucks’ brand guidelines and aesthetic. Through iterative prototyping and usability testing, I aimed to create a more intuitive and enjoyable app experience for everyday users.

Problem

Through research and user interviews, many people expressed that the app feels cluttered, navigation is unclear, and the design lacks consistency across screens. Common pain points included difficulties with ordering ahead, finding rewards, and customizing drinks—tasks that users found both frustrating and time-consuming.

Goals

  • Simplify navigation
  • Highlight rewards and key actions to improve engagement
  • Enhance accessibility and streamline the ordering process

Design Process

I followed a user-centered approach:

  • Conducted a heuristic analysis of the existing app
  • Created wireframes for key user flows
  • Tested with mock users using prototypes (Figma)
  • Iterated designs based on usability feedback

Final Designs

The new design makes ordering the main focus right on the home screen, so users don’t get distracted by ads or promos. There’s a simple bottom navigation bar to help people get around the app easily. Outcome

The redesign addresses key pain points identified through research, offering a cleaner navigation structure and simplified ordering process. By focusing on usability and brand consistency, the new design is expected to enhance user satisfaction, reduce friction in key tasks like ordering and rewards access, and ultimately encourage more frequent app use.
